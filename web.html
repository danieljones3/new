The next appearance of a two-wheeled riding machine was in 1865,
when pedals were applied directly to the front wheel.
This machine was known as the velocipede
(meaning "fast foot") as well as the "bone shaker,"
since it's wooden structure combined with the cobblestone roads
of the day made for an extremely uncomfortable ride.
They also became a fad and indoor riding academies,
similar to roller rinks, could be found in large cities.
